The Missing Math Class: Circular Operations

🤯 What They Never Taught You

Circular quantities are everywhere, but traditional math classes skip the operations entirely! Here's what you can actually do once you embed them as vectors:

📊 Basic Operations

Addition: v₁ + v₂
Compose two rotations
Subtraction: v₁ - v₂
Find angular difference
Average: mean(vectors)
Circular mean (always works!)
Interpolation: slerp(v₁, v₂, t)
Smooth rotation between angles

🧮 Advanced Operations

Distance: shortest_path(θ₁, θ₂)
Minimal angular separation
Scaling: scalar × vector
Scale magnitude, preserve direction
Derivatives: d/dt [cos(ωt), sin(ωt)]
Angular velocity vectors
Integration: ∫ ω(t) dt
Accumulate rotations over time

📈 Circular Statistics (The Really Cool Stuff)

# Operations that actually work on circular data!
angles = [350°, 10°, 5°, 355°]  # Wind directions
vectors = [to_vector(θ) for θ in angles]

# Circular mean (not 180°!)
mean_vector = np.mean(vectors, axis=0) 
mean_angle = from_vector(mean_vector)  # ≈ 0°

# Circular standard deviation  
dispersion = 1 - |mean_vector|  # How "spread out" are they?

# Circular correlation
correlation = correlate_circular(wind_dir, wave_dir)

# Circular regression (yes, this exists!)
slope, intercept = circular_regression(time, wave_directions)

💡 The Big Insight

Every operation you know from linear algebra works on circular quantities—you just need to embed them first! This is why embedding is such a powerful mathematical technique.

Circular Quantity → Vector → Linear Operation → Vector → Circular Result
